Key Accessibility Findings

The interactive dashboard above presents visual insights from a survey focused on digital accessibility challenges experienced by neurodivergent users. Below is a summary of the main findings drawn from both quantitative and qualitative data:

  • Daily Usage: All participants reported using websites or applications daily, emphasising the importance of inclusive design in everyday digital environments.
  • Common Barriers: Major challenges included cluttered layouts, excessive animations, inconsistent navigation, and overwhelming menus - all of which increase cognitive load.
  • Assistive Technology: Most participants did not use assistive tools like screen readers, suggesting a mismatch between conventional aids and neurodivergent needs.
  • Customisation Desires: There was strong interest in simple personalisation options such as dark mode, larger fonts, and content simplification - features often missing from mainstream platforms.
  • Textual Insights: Word clouds revealed a preference for clarity, ease of use, and familiarity. Words like easy, use, and clear were prominent, reflecting a desire for intuitive and low-effort experiences.
  • Awareness & Frustration: Participants expressed frustration with misconceptions about autism and ADHD, particularly regarding invisible symptoms and inconsistent support.

These insights directly informed the accessibility features demonstrated throughout this site and were mapped against WCAG 2.1/2.2 standards to ensure relevance and alignment with best practices.
